Surfacing Post Errors Case Study

Project Overview

As a part of a larger project to tackle post reliability, the Surfacing Post Errors project served a dual purpose of providing our customers with more information through transparency on post failure and of providing our internal team a breakdown of said post failures to tackle in future phases.

Teams

  • Product
  • Engineering
  • Marketing
  • Customer Success
  • Sales

Role

Sole UX/UI Designer

Key Points of Consideration

  • How to translate the technical post error messages appropriately for customer viewing
  • Design with technical restrictions and future improvements in mind inclusive of character limitations due to space, functionality given the legacy code, and the different areas of implementation
  • How to add value and maintain trust with our customers

Research

The problem we were addressing was that of post reliability. The goal was to bring up post reliability to 98% success rates from a starting point of below 50%. The bulk of the research and the initial work lied within the code and how we were tracking and organizing the post failures and their reasons. With the failures categorized, the team moved to group and translate the errors into colloquial language that our customers would understand. By surfacing post errors, we aimed to

  1. Provide transparency
  2. Clean up workflows for reposting content
  3. Gain metrics to better serve our customers

Ideation

User Experience and Feasability

During conversations of the ideal user experience, I ran into new information that disclosed a feasability barrier. I was working on the assumption that our users would be able to get immediate feedback on the action they had taken whether it was editing content to fit requirements or renewing a credential. This immediate feedback would allow for the post to be rescheduled or reposted from the modal through the access of an action button. However, given constraints, we were unable to provide that capability at the time. This shifted user experience flows as well as the visual design. It did complicate and elongate the process for the user.

Iterations

A few key iterations for this project include that of a change caused by feasability restrictions that surfaced after the beginning of the research and design process. The initial experience for the post errors project relied on the premise that once the user has addressed the error through the button entry point in the modal, that action taken would be reflected automatically. The button would change into a green checkmark icon which reflects the resolved error. However, during conversations with the engineering team, it was disclosed that to have said functionality, the lift would be larger than initially understood. Weighing out the factors of time, resource, and value, we decided upon an initial release of a manual process with plans of creating necessary endpoints for automation for a later update.

The iterated design is reflected below. The experience became manual in that once an error was addressed, the user would have to check the error message off. Upon checking off the item, the button, which acts as an entry point, would become disabled.

Having addressed feasability and fuctionality with regards to user experience. We did have a few iterations of the placement of specific elements. One of the decisions was in relation to the location of the checkbox icon. Through internal A/B testing and external A/B testing, we came to the conclusion of the third version with the checkbox to the right and separate from the card that holds the message, the button, and the support link.

Shown below, is the final design. Additional visual changes and behavioral changes were applied.

  1. The checkbox, when unchecked, is an empty square.
  2. The button is disabled once the checkbox is checked.
  3. The error message is strikedthrough once the checkbox is checked.

Result and Reflection

After the release, we received many postive responses with regards to the usefulness of the surface level errors. Through the transparency, customers are able to pinpoint aspects of their content prep that cause post failure. For post failures beyond the control of Cloud Campaign, we are able to provide more immediate awareness of the issue. This shortens the amount of time our customers are in the dark.
This project highlighted the importance of communication whether it was with internal team members, customers, and leadership.
